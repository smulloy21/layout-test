Osip Mandelstam - The Wolf poem

Published in 1931 in Tristia

See Original

For the thundering valor of ages to come,

For the towering tribe of man,

At the feast of my fathers I was deprived of my cup,

My happiness and my good name.


The age of the wolfhound was launched on my shouders

But I am no wolf by blood.

I'd rather be stuffed like a hat in the sleeve

Of Siberia's thick furry coat,


So as not to see cowardice, or gross slurping mud,

Or the bloody bones on the wheel,

So that all night the blue arctic foxes will shine

In their primeval beauty for me.


Lead me to the night where the Yenisei flows,

And the pines reach up to the stars,

Because I am not a wolf by blood

And only an equal will kill me.
